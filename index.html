TL;DR: We propose Uni-VisTA, a generalist model for 3D vision-language understanding, capable of taking various prompts to perform a wide range of tasks in a 3D Scene.

Abstract

A generalist model for 3D vision-language understanding is expected to smoothly integrate various scene representations and simultaneously perform a wide range of tasks with diverse inputs and outputs in a 3D scene. However, a considerable gap exists between existing methods and such a generalist model. To address this gap, we introduce Uni-VisTA, a versatile model capable of taking various prompts to tackle a wide range of 3D-VL tasks, from low-level instance segmentation to high-level reasoning and planning. This is achieved through three key innovations: (1) synchronization of different scene representations (i.e., voxels, point clouds, RGB-D images) into a common 3D coordinate space, (2) an attention-based query decoder for task-specific information retrieval, and (3) universal output heads for versatile task outputs.

Tested across ten diverse 3D-VL datasets, Uni-VisTA demonstrates superior performance on all tasks, setting new records on nine out of ten benchmarks and showing remarkable zero-shot capabilities with novel prompt types. Particularly, Uni-VisTA boosts the state-of-the-art on ScanNet200 by 1.8% (AP), ScanRefer by 5.8% (acc@0.5), Multi3DRef by 11.9% (F1@0.5), and Scan2Cap by 7.8% (CIDEr@0.5).

Contribution

Our main contributions are

  1. Uni-VisTA model, a unified model adept at han dling a broad spectrum of 3D vision-language tasks, rang- ing from low-level instance segmentation to high-level reasoning and planning.
  2. Representations alignment. Our model uniquely aligns various scene representations into a shared 3D space and employs an attention-based query decoder to adaptively extract task-relevant features, offering a cohesive approach to 3D-VL tasks RGB-D scans of 1,185 unique indoor scenes..
  3. Performance. In our extensive experimentation across various 3D-VL datasets, UNI-VISTA not only achieves competitive results but also sets new records in most of the tasks and zero-shot prompting capability, underscoring its effectiveness and versatility.
